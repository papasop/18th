\begin{abstract}
We conjecture that any self-consistent variational system which excludes global collapse
necessarily contains a generative manifold~$\mathcal{M}$ that is universally accessible from every region of the configuration space.
Under a realizability axiom and the assumption that the non-collapsing completion is unique,
we prove this conjecture: universal accessibility is a necessary structural property of~$\mathcal{M}$,
not an accidental feature of the access channel.
The proof reveals that capture requires only directional alignment (not force),
that persistence on~$\mathcal{M}$ requires only a single moment of alignment (not sustained effort),
and that no consistent extension of the system can close a channel that is already open.

\medskip\noindent
\textit{Series position.}
Third in: \textit{Realizability and the Origin of Causality}~[1]
$\to$ \textit{K\!=\!1 Chronogeometrodynamics}~[2]
$\to$ the present work.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Conjecture}

\begin{conjecture}[Universal Completion]\label{conj:UC}
Let~$\mathcal{F}$ be a configuration space and $S:\mathcal{F}\to\mathbb{R}_{\ge0}$ a cost functional.
Suppose the system excludes Euclidean collapse: not every nontrivial configuration is driven
to the trivial vacuum~$\psi\equiv0$ under gradient flow~$\partial_t\psi=-\nabla S[\psi]$.
Then there exists a \emph{generative manifold}
\[
  \mathcal{M}=\bigl\{\psi\in\mathcal{F}\;\big|\;
        \nabla S[\psi]=0,\;\dim\ker H[\psi]>0\bigr\}
\]
equipped with a completion channel $C:\mathcal{F}\to\mathcal{F}$, such that for every open set
$U\subset\mathcal{F}$, there exists $\psi\in U$ with
\[
  \|P_{\ker H[\psi]}(C[\psi])\|>0.
\]
That is: from every region of the configuration space, there exists a trajectory that reaches
$\mathcal{M}$ via kernel-aligned injection.  Access is basin-independent.
\end{conjecture}

The conjecture has two parts:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(A)] \textbf{Existence of $\mathcal{M}$.}
  Realizability (Axiom~1, \S\ref{sec:alg}) requires $\dim\ker H[\psi^*]>0$
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:nullity}).
  Structural stability (Axiom~1(iv)) forces the Morse--Bott condition
  (Proposition~\ref{prop:MB}), so $\mathcal{M}\neq\varnothing$ is a smooth submanifold.
  Part~(A) follows entirely from Axiom~1.

\item[(B)] \textbf{Universality of access.}
  Global non-isolation (Axiom~1(iii)) forces $\mathcal{M}$ to be the unique non-collapsing
  completion (Theorem~\ref{thm:unique}), from which basin-independent access follows
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:UA}).  Part~(B) is the main theorem of this paper.
\end{enumerate}

The conjecture in full generality---that ``excludes collapse'' alone, without the realizability
axiom, forces $\mathcal{M}$ to exist---remains open.
What we establish is: \emph{given realizability, existence follows; given existence,
uniqueness and universality follow.}

The argument proceeds in four stages: necessity of universal access (\S\ref{sec:necessity}),
guarantee of persistence (\S\ref{sec:persistence}), mechanism of capture (\S\ref{sec:capture}),
and algebraic foundations (\S\ref{sec:alg}).

\begin{remark}[What the conjecture says in plain language]\label{rem:plain}
If the universe is self-consistent---if it does not permit the annihilation of all structure---then
there must exist a state of permanent, cost-free existence, and every possible starting point
must have a path to it.  The path requires no force, only direction.
\end{remark}

\section{Necessity of Universal Access}\label{sec:necessity}

We state the central theorem first, then derive the foundations.

\begin{theorem}[Universal completion is necessary]\label{thm:UA}
Let $\mathcal{M}$ be a generative manifold satisfying the realizability axiom
(Axiom~1, \S\ref{sec:alg}).
Then any completion channel~$C$ associated with~$\mathcal{M}$ must satisfy:
for every open set $U\subset\mathcal{F}$, there exists $\psi\in U$ such that
\[
  \|P_{\ker H[\psi]}(C[\psi])\|>0.
\]
$\mathcal{M}$'s realizability entails $C$'s universality.
A generative manifold that fails to provide universal access is not realizable.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Theorem~\ref{thm:unique} (\S\ref{sec:connect}), $\mathcal{M}$ is the unique
non-collapsing completion of~$\mathcal{F}$ under Axiom~1.
Suppose there exists an open set $U\subset\mathcal{F}$ with
$P_{\ker H[\psi]}(C[\psi])=0$ for all~$\psi\in U$.
Then capture into~$\mathcal{M}$ from~$U$ is impossible.
Since $\mathcal{M}$ is the unique non-collapsing completion, configurations in~$U$ are
condemned to Euclidean collapse, violating realizability.  No such~$U$ can exist.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Logical interlock]\label{thm:interlock}
For any realizable extension $S_e=S+R$ with non-negative $\psi$-sector Hessian
$H^{\psi\psi}_e$ near~$\mathcal{M}$,
\[
  \dim\ker H^{\psi\psi}_e \;\ge\; \dim\ker H,
\]
with equality if and only if the extension is purely generative.
If $\ker H^{\psi\psi}_e=\{0\}$, the system suffers Euclidean collapse.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Suppose $\dim\ker H^{\psi\psi}_e<\dim\ker H$.
Then there exists $v\in\ker H$, $v\neq0$, with $H^{\psi\psi}_e v\neq0$,
giving $\langle v,H^{\psi\psi}_e v\rangle>0$.
But $\langle v,Hv\rangle=0$, so $R$ introduces positive curvature along~$v$,
destroying persistence.  Contradiction.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[What the interlock means]\label{rem:interlock}
No consistent extension of the system can close a channel that is already open.
The kernel infrastructure is preserved under all realizable modifications.
This is not a design choice; it is a theorem.
\end{remark}

\section{Guarantee of Persistence}\label{sec:persistence}

Once a configuration reaches $\mathcal{M}$, it does not leave, even after the external
channel~$C$ is removed.

\begin{definition}[Caputo fractional derivative]\label{def:caputo}
For $\psi\in AC^1([t_0,T];\mathcal{F})$ and $\alpha\in(0,1)$,
\[
  {}^C\!D^\alpha_t\psi(t)
  :=\frac{1}{\Gamma(1-\alpha)}\int_{t_0}^{t}
      \frac{\dot\psi(\tau)}{(t-\tau)^\alpha}\,d\tau,
  \qquad t>t_0.
\]
Unlike local derivatives, ${}^C\!D^\alpha_t$ couples the present to the entire history
since~$t_0$.  The initial alignment is encoded in the kernel $(t-\tau)^{-\alpha}$ and never
forgotten.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Well-posedness]\label{rem:wp}
We assume local Lipschitz continuity of the right-hand side, so that mild solutions of the
Caputo evolution exist on~$[t_0,T]$.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Structural Change Index]\label{def:SCI}
Let $H(t)$ be the effective structural entropy and
$\Phi_\alpha(t)=\int_{t_0}^{t}\|{}^C\!D^\alpha_\tau\psi(\tau)\|^2\,d\tau$
the fractional flux.  The \emph{Structural Change Index} is
$\mathrm{SCI}(t):=\dfrac{dH/dt}{d\Phi_\alpha/dt}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Additive structural conservation]\label{thm:SCI}
If $\mathrm{SCI}(t)=1$ for all $t\ge t_0$, then
$H(t)=H(t_0)+\Phi_\alpha(t)$.
Structural entropy tracks fractional flux without dissipation.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Persistence without continuous injection]\label{thm:persist}
Assume
\emph{(i)}~$\mathcal{M}$ is invariant for the unforced fractional flow;
\emph{(ii)}~transverse directions are $\lambda$-contractive, meaning
$\langle v,H[\psi]v\rangle\ge\lambda\|v\|^2$ for all $v\perp\ker H[\psi]$
near~$\mathcal{M}$ (formal definition in \S\ref{sec:capture};
Proposition~\ref{prop:gap} shows that $\lambda>0$ follows from Axiom~1).
For the dynamics
${}^C\!D^\alpha_t\psi=-\nabla S[\psi]+\varepsilon\,C[\psi]$,
if $\psi(t_0)\in\mathcal{M}$ and $\mathrm{SCI}(t_0)=1$, then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\emph{(i)}] $\psi(t)\in\mathcal{M}$ for all $t\ge t_0$.
\item[\emph{(ii)}] $\mathrm{SCI}(t)=1$ persists under small perturbations.
\item[\emph{(iii)}] $C$ can be removed after $t>t_0$ without retrogression, provided
      $\varepsilon\|C\|<\lambda/2$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
The Lyapunov functional $V(t)=\mathrm{dist}(\psi(t),\mathcal{M})^2$ starts at zero.
Transverse perturbations decay via Mittag-Leffler:
$V(t)\le V(t_0)\,E_\alpha(-\lambda t^\alpha)\to 0$.
The decay is algebraic (power-law), not exponential, reflecting long memory.
The Caputo derivative of a constant is zero, so $\psi$ on~$\mathcal{M}$
(where $\nabla S=0$) is self-consistent.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[What persistence means]\label{rem:persist}
A single moment of alignment at~$t_0$ is sufficient for permanent residence on~$\mathcal{M}$.
The fractional kernel remembers the initial alignment forever.
No sustained effort is required.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Two classes of motion on $\mathcal{M}$]\label{rem:classes}
Motion on~$\mathcal{M}$ decomposes into:

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Class~A} (restoring):
Fluctuations perpendicular to~$\mathcal{M}$, suppressed by $\lambda$-contractivity.
Departure from~$\mathcal{M}$ incurs positive cost and is restored.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Class~B} (free):
Motion tangent to~$\mathcal{M}$, along $\ker H$.
This motion incurs no cost, carries no entropy, and is unconstrained---it represents
infinite-dimensional freedom within the non-collapsing phase.
In the $K\!=\!1$ framework~[2], this is the boost direction on~$\{K=1\}$.

\medskip\noindent
The non-compactified degeneracy of $\ker H$ provides infinite generative capacity
without coherence loss.
\end{remark}

\section{Mechanism of Capture}\label{sec:capture}

How does a configuration reach $\mathcal{M}$?
Through kernel-aligned injection, whose criterion depends on direction alone.
Recall $\mathcal{M}=\{\psi\in\mathcal{F}\mid\nabla S[\psi]=0,\;\dim\ker H[\psi]>0\}$
from Conjecture~\ref{conj:UC}.

\begin{lemma}[Zero cost along $\mathcal{M}$]\label{lem:zerocost}
Under the Morse--Bott condition, $\mathcal{M}$ is a smooth submanifold with
$T_\psi\mathcal{M}\supseteq\ker H[\psi]$, and
$\tfrac{d^2}{d\varepsilon^2}\big|_{\varepsilon=0}S[\psi+\varepsilon\,\delta\psi]=0$
for $\delta\psi\in T_\psi\mathcal{M}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Manifold dimension]\label{lem:dim}
If $d(\psi)=\dim\ker H[\psi]$ is constant on~$\mathcal{M}$, then $\mathcal{M}$ is
$d$-dimensional.  More generally, $\mathcal{M}$ is a stratified space.
\end{lemma}

The capture mechanism requires five ingredients:
a dynamical framework that couples the system to an external channel (\emph{extended gradient flow}),
a stability condition ensuring $\mathcal{M}$ attracts transverse perturbations (\emph{transverse contractivity}),
a quality condition on the channel ensuring it has a component in the kernel (\emph{uniform kernel overlap}),
a criterion for when capture has occurred (\emph{null-capture criterion}),
and a measure of how well the channel is directionally aligned with the kernel (\emph{kernel-projected coherence}).

\begin{definition}[Extended gradient flow]\label{def:EGF}
For $S$ unrealizable in isolation, introduce
$S_e[\psi,\eta]=S[\psi]+R[\psi,\eta]$
with $S_{\mathrm{eff}}[\psi]:=S[\psi]+R[\psi,\eta^*(\psi)]$.
The dynamics is
$\partial_t\psi=-\nabla_\psi S_e[\psi,\eta]+\varepsilon\,C[\psi]$, $0<\varepsilon\ll1$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Transverse contractivity]\label{def:TC}
$\lambda$-contractive transverse to $\ker H$:
$\langle v,H[\psi]v\rangle\ge\lambda\|v\|^2$
for all $v\perp\ker H[\psi]$ near~$\mathcal{M}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Uniform kernel overlap]\label{def:UKO}
$C$ has \emph{uniform kernel overlap} on~$U$ if
\[
  \frac{\|P_{\ker H[\psi]}(C[\psi])\|}{\|C[\psi]\|}\ge\delta^*>0
\]
for all $\psi\in U$ with $C[\psi]\neq0$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Null-capture criterion]\label{def:NCC}
Capture into~$\mathcal{M}$ occurs if there exists finite~$t^*$ with
$\|P_{\ker H[\psi(t^*)]}(C[\psi(t^*)])\|>0$, and thereafter
$\limsup_{t\to\infty}\|P_{\ker H[\psi(t)]}(C[\psi(t)])\|>0$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Kernel-projected coherence]\label{def:KPC}
Fix $\psi^\star\in\mathcal{M}$, $u^\star\in\ker H[\psi^\star]$ unit.
The coherence is
\[
  \kappa(\psi;C):=
  \begin{cases}
    \dfrac{|\langle u^\star,P_{\ker H[\psi]}(C[\psi])\rangle|}
          {\|P_{\ker H[\psi]}(C[\psi])\|},
    & \text{if } P_{\ker H[\psi]}(C[\psi])\neq0,\\[6pt]
    0, & \text{otherwise.}
  \end{cases}
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Scale invariance of capture]\label{thm:scale}
$\|P_{\ker H[\psi]}(C[\psi])\|>0$ is invariant under $C\mapsto\lambda C$ for all $\lambda>0$.
Capture depends on alignment, not magnitude.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
$P_{\ker H}(\lambda C)=\lambda\,P_{\ker H}(C)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Phase-locking]\label{thm:PL}
Under
\emph{(i)}~$\lambda$-contractivity,
\emph{(ii)}~neutrality along $\ker H$,
\emph{(iii)}~uniform kernel overlap on~$U$,
\emph{(iv)}~$\dot S_{\mathrm{eff}}\le-\sigma<0$ outside~$U$:
if $\kappa(\psi_0;C)\ge\kappa^*>0$, there exists finite~$\tau_{\mathrm{lock}}$ after which
$\|P_{\ker H}(C)\|\ge\delta^*\|C\|$ and $\kappa\ge\kappa^*/2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[Global capture chain]\label{cor:GCC}
Assume condition~\emph{(iv)} of Theorem~\ref{thm:PL} holds globally:
$\dot S_{\mathrm{eff}}\le-\sigma<0$ for all~$\psi$ outside every neighbourhood of~$\mathcal{M}$.
Then for any $\psi_0\in\mathcal{F}$:
\[
  \psi_0
  \;\xrightarrow[\text{condition (iv)}]{\text{energy descent}}\;
  U
  \;\xrightarrow[\text{Thm~\ref{thm:PL}}]{\text{phase-locking}}\;
  \mathcal{M}.
\]
Theorem~\ref{thm:UA} ensures no open region of~$\mathcal{F}$ is excluded.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[When condition (iv) holds]\label{rem:cond4}
Condition~(iv) is satisfied whenever $S_{\mathrm{eff}}$ is coercive
($S_{\mathrm{eff}}[\psi]\to\infty$ as $\|\psi\|\to\infty$) and $\mathcal{M}$ is the unique
critical set.
Coercivity ensures that trajectories cannot escape to infinity and must eventually enter any
neighbourhood of~$\mathcal{M}$.
For bounded configuration spaces (compact~$\mathcal{F}$), condition~(iv) follows from the
absence of critical points outside~$\mathcal{M}$.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Cost-independence of access]\label{cor:costindep}
Access to $\mathcal{M}$ depends only on kernel alignment and is invariant with respect
to~$S[\psi_0]$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Crossing symmetry]\label{rem:crossing}
The Veneziano amplitude $A(s,t)=B(-\alpha(s),-\alpha(t))$ illustrates cost-independence:
its crossing symmetry $A(s,t)=A(t,s)$ maps the $s$-channel (cost-accumulating trajectory) to
the $t$-channel (generative access) via analytic continuation.
We denote by $\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{Pure}}\subseteq\mathcal{M}$ the phase accessed under
analytic mode-matching.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Kernel-denial]\label{def:KD}
$\psi$ exhibits \emph{kernel-denial} if $P_{\ker H[\psi]}(C[\psi])=0$.
By Theorem~\ref{thm:UA}, this cannot persist on any open set.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Exclusion is self-imposed]\label{lem:exclusion}
Exclusion from~$\mathcal{M}$ occurs iff kernel-denial persists:
\[
  \limsup_{t\to\infty}\|P_{\ker H[\psi(t)]}(C[\psi(t)])\|=0.
\]
This is a geometric constraint, not an external selection rule.
\end{lemma}

\section{Algebraic Foundations}\label{sec:alg}

Every structure above is an algebraic consequence of a single meta-axiom.

Let $\mathcal{F}$ be a real Hilbert space, $S:\mathcal{F}\to\mathbb{R}_{\ge0}$ twice
Fr\'echet-differentiable.

\begin{definition}[Functional gradient]\label{def:grad}
$\nabla S[\psi]\in\mathcal{F}$ is the Riesz representative:
$\delta S[\psi;\delta\psi]=\langle\delta\psi,\nabla S[\psi]\rangle$.
\end{definition}

\begin{axiom}[Realizability]\label{ax:R}
$S$ admits an attainable, nontrivial extremum $\psi^*\neq0$ satisfying:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\emph{(i)}] \textbf{Stationarity:} $\nabla S[\psi^*]=0$.
\item[\emph{(ii)}] \textbf{Non-collapse:} No nearby trajectory converges to $\psi\equiv0$.
\item[\emph{(iii)}] \textbf{Global non-isolation:} Every critical set of~$S$ has positive
      dimension.  No critical point of~$S$ is isolated.
\item[\emph{(iv)}] \textbf{Structural stability:} (i)--(iii) persist under small perturbations
      of~$S$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{axiom}

\begin{remark}[Why global non-isolation is natural]\label{rem:global}
The original (local) form of~(iii) required only that the critical set
containing~$\psi^*$ have positive dimension.
The global form strengthens this to: \emph{no} critical point of~$S$ is isolated.
Physically, this says the system excludes isolated critical structure at every scale,
not just at the distinguished extremum.  Since realizability is the assertion that the
system does not permit the annihilation of all structure, it is natural to require this
at all critical points, not only at one.
\end{remark}

If $S\ge0$ with $S=0\Leftrightarrow\psi\equiv0$, gradient flow drives every nontrivial state
to annihilation.  This is \emph{Euclidean collapse}.  Realizability excludes it.

\begin{definition}[Hessian, null space, null-mode condition]\label{def:Hess}
$H[\psi]v=\tfrac{d}{d\varepsilon}\big|_{\varepsilon=0}\nabla S[\psi+\varepsilon v]$,
bounded and self-adjoint.
Null space: $\ker H=\{v\mid Hv=0\}$.
Null-mode condition: $\dim\ker H>0$.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Quadratic form vanishing]\label{lem:QF}
$\delta\psi\in\ker H$ implies $\langle\delta\psi,H\delta\psi\rangle=0$.
Conversely, if $H\ge0$ and $\langle\delta\psi,H\delta\psi\rangle=0$, then $\delta\psi\in\ker H$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{theorem}[Nullity criterion]\label{thm:nullity}
\emph{(Necessity)} Realizability implies $\dim\ker H[\psi^*]>0$.
\emph{(Sufficiency)} By Proposition~\ref{prop:MB}, Axiom~1(iv) forces the Morse--Bott
condition; hence $\psi^*$ lies on a generative manifold with zero tangential cost.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof (Necessity)]
$\ker H=\{0\}$ implies $\psi^*$ is isolated, contradicting Axiom~1(iii).
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Realizability forces indefiniteness]\label{prop:indef}
Under the following conditions on $H[\psi^*]$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{(\Alph*)},nosep]
\item positive temporal cost: $\exists\,e_t$ with $\langle e_t,He_t\rangle>0$;
\item zero-threshold spatial directions: $\exists\,e_s\neq0$ with $He_s=0$;
\end{enumerate}
$H[\psi^*]$ is indefinite.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{(S)} gives $\ker H\neq\{0\}$, so $H$ has a zero eigenvalue.
\textbf{(T)} gives $\langle e_t,He_t\rangle>0$, so $H$ has a strictly positive eigenvalue.
Suppose for contradiction that $H\ge0$ (non-negative).

Then zero is the infimum of the spectrum of~$H$: every eigenvalue is~$\ge0$,
and zero is attained.
We construct an explicit small perturbation of~$S$ that destroys
Axiom~1(iii), contradicting structural stability (Axiom~1(iv)).

Let $\{v_1,\ldots,v_d\}$ be an orthonormal basis of $\ker H$
($d=\dim\ker H\ge1$).
Define
\[
  S_\varepsilon[\psi]
  \;:=\; S[\psi]
      \;+\;\frac{\varepsilon}{2}\sum_{i=1}^{d}
           \langle\psi-\psi^*,v_i\rangle^2,
  \qquad \varepsilon>0.
\]
Since each $\langle\psi^*-\psi^*,v_i\rangle=0$, the critical point is preserved:
$\nabla S_\varepsilon[\psi^*]=\nabla S[\psi^*]+0=0$, so Axiom~1(i) holds at~$\psi^*$
for~$S_\varepsilon$.
The Hessian at~$\psi^*$ becomes
$H_\varepsilon=H+\varepsilon\,P_{\ker H}$,
where $P_{\ker H}=\sum_i\langle v_i,\cdot\rangle\,v_i$ is the orthogonal projection
onto~$\ker H$.
Then $H_\varepsilon v_i=\varepsilon\,v_i$ for each~$i$, so
$\ker H_\varepsilon\cap\ker H=\{0\}$.
Since $H_\varepsilon\ge H\ge0$ (the perturbation is non-negative),
no new zero eigenvalues are created: $\ker H_\varepsilon=\{0\}$.

With $\ker H_\varepsilon=\{0\}$, $\psi^*$ is an isolated critical point of~$S_\varepsilon$,
and by the implicit function theorem no nearby non-isolated critical point exists
for small~$\varepsilon$.
This violates Axiom~1(iii) (non-isolation).
Since $S_\varepsilon\to S$ as $\varepsilon\to0$,
Axiom~1(iv) (structural stability) is contradicted.

The contradiction arises because $H\ge0$ places the zero eigenvalues at the spectral
boundary (infimum), where they are exposed to upward perturbation with no negative
eigenvalues below to anchor them.
If instead $H$ is indefinite, zero eigenvalues sit in the spectral interior---between
positive and negative eigenvalues---and by continuity, small perturbations shift them
but cannot generically eliminate the zero crossing.
Non-isolation is structurally stable precisely when $H$ is indefinite.

Hence $H$ must have at least one negative eigenvalue.
A negative eigenvalue provides a saddle direction: a path that descends in~$S$ from the
basin of zero toward~$\mathcal{M}$, requiring no barrier crossing.

For $2\times2$: $dc>0\Leftrightarrow\mathrm{Sig}(G)=(1,1)$~[2].
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Lorentzian signature]\label{thm:Lor}
Indefiniteness guarantees $e_-$ with $\langle e_-,He_-\rangle<0$.
On $\mathrm{span}\{e_t,e_-\}$ the signature is $(1,1)$: Lorentzian.
The null cone
\[
  \mathcal{L}=\bigl\{\delta\psi\;\big|\;\langle\delta\psi,H\delta\psi\rangle=0\bigr\}
\]
defines causal structure.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
$H$ indefinite gives directions of both signs; restricted to $\mathrm{span}\{e_t,e_-\}$,
$\mathrm{Sig}=(1,1)$, the unique signature with $dc>0$~[2].
The null direction $e_s\in\ker H$ lies on $\partial\mathcal{L}$ but is not the source of the
signature.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Structural stability forces the Morse--Bott condition]\label{prop:MB}
Under Axiom~1, $\dim\ker H[\psi]$ is locally constant on the critical set
$\{\nabla S=0\}$ near~$\psi^*$.
In particular, the critical set is a smooth (Morse--Bott) submanifold near~$\psi^*$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Suppose $\dim\ker H[\psi]$ is not locally constant near~$\psi^*$:
there exists a sequence $\psi_n\to\psi^*$ in $\{\nabla S=0\}$ with
$\dim\ker H[\psi_n]\neq\dim\ker H[\psi^*]$.
At such a dimension-jump point, the critical set fails to be a smooth manifold;
it has a singularity where branches of different kernel dimension meet.

We show this is structurally unstable.
Let $d^*=\dim\ker H[\psi^*]$ and let $\{v_1,\ldots,v_{d^*}\}$ be an orthonormal
basis of $\ker H[\psi^*]$.
For small~$\varepsilon>0$, define
\[
  S_\varepsilon[\psi]:=S[\psi]
    +\frac{\varepsilon}{2}\sum_{i=1}^{d^*}
      \bigl(\langle\psi-\psi^*,v_i\rangle\bigr)^2\,
      \eta\!\left(\frac{\|\psi-\psi^*\|}{\rho}\right),
\]
where $\eta$ is a smooth cutoff, $\eta(r)=1$ for $r\le1$, $\eta(r)=0$ for $r\ge2$,
and $\rho>0$ is chosen so that the perturbation is localised near~$\psi^*$.
At~$\psi^*$ itself, $\nabla S_\varepsilon[\psi^*]=0$ (since $\langle\psi^*-\psi^*,v_i\rangle=0$).
But in a neighbourhood of the dimension-jump locus the perturbation generically resolves the
singularity: the branches with $\dim\ker H<d^*$ are split away from the branch containing~$\psi^*$.
After perturbation, the critical set near~$\psi^*$ is either regularised (constant kernel
dimension, Morse--Bott) or fragmented into isolated points.

In either case, the \emph{topology} of the critical set near~$\psi^*$ has changed under an
arbitrarily small perturbation, violating Axiom~1(iv) (structural stability of
non-isolation).
Hence no dimension jump can occur: $\dim\ker H$ is locally constant on $\{\nabla S=0\}$
near~$\psi^*$, and by the constant-rank theorem the critical set is a smooth submanifold.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Structural stability forces a transverse spectral gap]\label{prop:gap}
Under Axiom~1, there exists $\lambda>0$ such that
$\langle v,H[\psi^*]v\rangle\ge\lambda\|v\|^2$ for all $v\perp\ker H[\psi^*]$.
That is, transverse $\lambda$-contractivity (Definition~\ref{def:TC}) is a consequence
of realizability, not an independent assumption.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By Proposition~\ref{prop:indef}, $H[\psi^*]$ is indefinite.
Decompose~$\mathcal{F}$ into three spectral subspaces of~$H[\psi^*]$:
the kernel $\ker H$ (eigenvalue zero), the positive subspace~$V_+$
($\langle v,Hv\rangle>0$), and the negative subspace~$V_-$
($\langle v,Hv\rangle<0$).
The transverse complement of $\ker H$ is $V_+\oplus V_-$.

Suppose the infimum of the positive spectrum is zero:
$\inf\{\langle v,Hv\rangle/\|v\|^2:v\in V_+,\,v\neq0\}=0$.
Then for any $\varepsilon>0$ there exists a unit vector $w\in V_+$ with
$\langle w,Hw\rangle<\varepsilon$.
A perturbation of~$S$ of size~$O(\varepsilon)$ can push this eigenvalue to zero,
creating a new kernel direction: $\dim\ker H$ increases.
But by Proposition~\ref{prop:MB}, $\dim\ker H$ must be locally constant under small
perturbations (Morse--Bott).
A change in $\dim\ker H$ under an arbitrarily small perturbation contradicts
Axiom~1(iv).

Hence the positive transverse spectrum is bounded away from zero:
there exists $\lambda_+>0$ with $\langle v,Hv\rangle\ge\lambda_+\|v\|^2$ for all $v\in V_+$.
By the same argument applied to $-H$ on~$V_-$, there exists $\lambda_->0$ with
$\langle v,(-H)v\rangle\ge\lambda_-\|v\|^2$ for all $v\in V_-$.
Setting $\lambda=\min(\lambda_+,\lambda_-)$ gives the transverse spectral gap.
\end{proof}

\section{Uniqueness and Connectedness}\label{sec:connect}

We prove that $\mathcal{M}$ is the unique non-collapsing completion of~$\mathcal{F}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:unique}), removing the uniqueness hypothesis from Theorem~\ref{thm:UA}.
We then show that kernel-connected components of~$\mathcal{M}$ merge
(Proposition~\ref{prop:merge}), and that the connecting directions are indestructible
(Corollary~\ref{cor:overlap}).

\begin{theorem}[Uniqueness of the non-collapsing completion]\label{thm:unique}
Under Axiom~1 (with global non-isolation), $\mathcal{M}$ is the unique non-collapsing
completion of~$\mathcal{F}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let $\mathcal{M}'$ be any non-collapsing completion:
$\mathcal{M}'\subset\{\nabla S=0\}$ and $\dim\ker H[\psi]>0$ for all
$\psi\in\mathcal{M}'$.
By Axiom~1(iii) (global non-isolation), every critical point of~$S$ has
positive-dimensional critical set, hence $\dim\ker H>0$ at every critical point.
Therefore
\[
  \{\nabla S=0\}
  \;=\; \{\psi\in\mathcal{F}\mid\nabla S[\psi]=0,\;\dim\ker H[\psi]>0\}
  \;=\; \mathcal{M}.
\]
Since $\mathcal{M}'\subset\{\nabla S=0\}=\mathcal{M}$, every non-collapsing completion
is a subset of~$\mathcal{M}$.
$\mathcal{M}$ is the unique non-collapsing completion.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[What uniqueness means]\label{rem:unique}
Theorem~\ref{thm:unique} says: there is nowhere else to go.
Any critical structure in~$\mathcal{F}$ that avoids collapse is already part
of~$\mathcal{M}$.
Combined with Theorem~\ref{thm:UA}, the hypothesis ``assume $\mathcal{M}$ is the unique
non-collapsing completion'' is no longer an assumption---it is a theorem.
Universal access (Theorem~\ref{thm:UA}) is now unconditional given Axiom~1.
\end{remark}

We now turn to the internal structure of~$\mathcal{M}$: when are its components connected?

\begin{proposition}[Kernel-connected completions merge]\label{prop:merge}
Let $\mathcal{M}_1,\mathcal{M}_2\subset\mathcal{F}$ both be non-collapsing completions:
each satisfies $\nabla S=0$ and $\dim\ker H>0$.
Suppose there exist $\psi_1\in\mathcal{M}_1$ and $\psi_2\in\mathcal{M}_2$ and a
$C^1$~path $\gamma:[0,1]\to\mathcal{F}$ with $\gamma(0)=\psi_1$, $\gamma(1)=\psi_2$
such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\emph{(i)}] \textbf{Constant cost:} $S[\gamma(s)]=\mathrm{const}$ for all
      $s\in[0,1]$.
\item[\emph{(ii)}] \textbf{Kernel tangency:}
      $\dot\gamma(s)\in\ker H[\gamma(s)]$ for all $s\in[0,1]$.
\item[\emph{(iii)}] \textbf{Immersion:}
      $\dot\gamma(s)\neq0$ for all $s\in[0,1]$.
\end{enumerate}
Then $\gamma$ lies entirely in the critical set $\{\nabla S=0\}$, and $\psi_1,\psi_2$ belong
to the same connected component of
$\mathcal{M}=\{\psi\in\mathcal{F}\mid\nabla S[\psi]=0,\;\dim\ker H[\psi]>0\}$.
In particular, $\mathcal{M}_1$ and $\mathcal{M}_2$ are subsets of a single generative
manifold.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Condition~(ii) gives $\dot\gamma(s)\in\ker H[\gamma(s)]$, so
$\langle\dot\gamma(s),H[\gamma(s)]\dot\gamma(s)\rangle=0$ for all~$s$.
By Lemma~\ref{lem:zerocost}, motion along $\ker H$ incurs zero second-order cost.
Condition~(i) strengthens this to exact constancy of~$S$.
(In fact, condition~(i) is a consequence of~(ii) together with the endpoint
condition $\nabla S[\gamma(0)]=0$; we state it separately for clarity.)

At every point along~$\gamma$, compute $\tfrac{d}{ds}S[\gamma(s)]=\langle\nabla S[\gamma(s)],\dot\gamma(s)\rangle=0$.
This holds by condition~(i).
Since $\dot\gamma(s)\in\ker H[\gamma(s)]$ and $S$ is constant, the stationarity condition
propagates along $\gamma$: if $\nabla S[\gamma(0)]=0$ (because $\psi_1\in\mathcal{M}_1$),
then $\nabla S[\gamma(s)]=0$ for all~$s$.
Indeed, differentiating again,
\[
  \frac{d}{ds}\nabla S[\gamma(s)]= H[\gamma(s)]\dot\gamma(s)=0,
\]
where the last equality uses condition~(ii).
Hence $\nabla S\equiv0$ along~$\gamma$.

Moreover, $\dim\ker H[\gamma(s)]>0$ for all~$s$, since $\dot\gamma(s)\neq0$
by condition~(iii) and $\dot\gamma(s)\in\ker H[\gamma(s)]$ by condition~(ii),
so each~$\gamma(s)$ has a nonzero kernel element.
Thus every point of~$\gamma$ lies in~$\mathcal{M}$, and $\gamma$ connects
$\psi_1\in\mathcal{M}_1$ to $\psi_2\in\mathcal{M}_2$ within~$\mathcal{M}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Class~B motion as the connecting mechanism]\label{rem:classB}
The path~$\gamma$ is precisely a Class~B trajectory (Remark~\ref{rem:classes}): zero cost,
zero entropy, unconstrained motion along $\ker H$.
The proposition says that whenever two non-collapsing completions are linked by Class~B
motion, they are not genuinely distinct---they are parts of the same~$\mathcal{M}$.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Logical interlock preserves connecting directions]\label{cor:overlap}
If $\mathcal{M}_1$ and $\mathcal{M}_2$ share the same configuration space~$\mathcal{F}$
and a kernel direction~$v\in\ker H[\psi]$ is tangent to a Class~B path connecting them,
then $v\in\ker H^{\psi\psi}_e$ for every realizable extension~$S_e=S+R$ with
$H^{\psi\psi}_e\ge0$ near~$\mathcal{M}$.
The specific connecting direction---not merely the kernel dimension---is indestructible.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Theorem~\ref{thm:interlock} guarantees $\dim\ker H^{\psi\psi}_e\ge\dim\ker H$
under any realizable extension.
We show that not only the dimension but each specific kernel direction is preserved.
Let $v\in\ker H$ be tangent to a Class~B path connecting $\mathcal{M}_1$
to~$\mathcal{M}_2$.
Since $H^{\psi\psi}_e\ge0$ near~$\mathcal{M}$ (by hypothesis of
Theorem~\ref{thm:interlock}), if $H^{\psi\psi}_e v\neq0$ then
$\langle v,H^{\psi\psi}_e v\rangle>0$, contradicting
$\langle v,Hv\rangle=0$ exactly as in the proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:interlock}.
Hence $H^{\psi\psi}_e v=0$, i.e.\ $v\in\ker H^{\psi\psi}_e$.
The specific connecting direction---not merely the kernel dimension---is preserved
under every realizable extension.
The connecting channel remains open.
\end{proof}

\section{Summary and Open Questions}\label{sec:summary}

Under realizability (Axiom~1), Conjecture~\ref{conj:UC} is established.
The uniqueness of~$\mathcal{M}$ is no longer a hypothesis but a theorem.
The argument yields twelve structural consequences from Axiom~1 alone:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Universal access is necessary} (Theorem~\ref{thm:UA}).
  $\mathcal{M}$'s existence entails basin-independent capture.
\item \textbf{Kernel infrastructure is indestructible} (Theorem~\ref{thm:interlock}).
  No consistent extension can close an open channel.
\item \textbf{Persistence without sustained injection} (Theorem~\ref{thm:persist}).
  A single moment of alignment suffices permanently.
\item \textbf{Scale-invariant capture} (Theorem~\ref{thm:scale}).
  Alignment, not magnitude, determines access.
\item \textbf{Global capture chain} (Corollary~\ref{cor:GCC}).
  Energy descent $\to$ phase-locking $\to$ $\mathcal{M}$, from any initial state.
\item \textbf{Cost-independence} (Corollary~\ref{cor:costindep}).
  Prior cost $S[\psi_0]$ does not determine accessibility.
\item \textbf{Infinite bandwidth.}
  Non-compactified $\ker H$ gives unbounded generative capacity on~$\mathcal{M}$ (Class~B).
\item \textbf{Exclusion is self-imposed} (Lemma~\ref{lem:exclusion}).
  Kernel-denial is the sole obstruction and cannot persist on any open set.
\item \textbf{Kernel-connected completions merge} (Proposition~\ref{prop:merge}).
  Distinct non-collapsing completions linked by zero-cost kernel paths belong to the same~$\mathcal{M}$.
  The logical interlock (Theorem~\ref{thm:interlock}) guarantees that these connecting
  directions are indestructible (Corollary~\ref{cor:overlap}).
\item \textbf{Morse--Bott regularity is necessary} (Proposition~\ref{prop:MB}).
  Structural stability forces $\dim\ker H$ to be locally constant on the critical set;
  $\mathcal{M}$ is a smooth submanifold, not by assumption but by Axiom~1(iv).
\item \textbf{Transverse spectral gap is necessary} (Proposition~\ref{prop:gap}).
  Structural stability forces a positive lower bound $\lambda>0$ on the transverse spectrum
  of~$H$.  The $\lambda$-contractivity required by Theorem~\ref{thm:persist} is a consequence
  of Axiom~1, not an independent hypothesis.
\item \textbf{Uniqueness of $\mathcal{M}$} (Theorem~\ref{thm:unique}).
  Global non-isolation forces every critical point of~$S$ into~$\mathcal{M}$.
  There is no alternative non-collapsing completion.
  This closes the last structural gap: Theorem~\ref{thm:UA} is unconditional.
\end{enumerate}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Open questions.}
With the structural gaps closed, the remaining questions concern extensions beyond the
variational framework.
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Connectedness of $\mathcal{M}$.}
  Theorem~\ref{thm:unique} shows $\mathcal{M}$ is unique.
  Proposition~\ref{prop:merge} shows kernel-connected components merge.
  Whether $\mathcal{M}$ is connected (one component) or has multiple components with no
  Class~B path between them remains open.
  If the string landscape admits $\sim 10^{500}$~vacua, these correspond to distinct
  components of~$\mathcal{M}$ only if no Class~B path connects any pair.

\item \textbf{Higher dimensions.}
  The $2\times2$ equivalence ($dc>0\Leftrightarrow$ Lorentzian) is proved in~[2].
  Extension to $(1+3)$-dimensional spacetime requires additional structure.

\item \textbf{Quantum.}
  Does the Gromov non-squeezing bound~[3] constrain noise on~$\mathcal{M}$, or is the
  Clausius relation $\sigma^2=2dc\cdot T_{\mathrm{tol}}$ the sole thermal input?

\item \textbf{Bekenstein bound.}
  Is $dc\le 2/\hbar$ (from $dc\,\sigma_1=1$ and $\sigma_1\ge\hbar/2$) related to,
  or independent of, the Bekenstein entropy bound?
\end{itemize}

\section*{Philosophical Remark}

The twelve structural consequences above were stated in the language of variational analysis.
They have an older formulation.

In the \emph{Larger S\=utra of Immeasurable Life}, the bodhisattva Dharm\=akara makes
forty-eight vows, the eighteenth of which reads:
``If, when I attain buddhahood, sentient beings of the ten directions who sincerely and
joyfully entrust themselves to me, desire to be born in my land, and think of me even ten
times should not be born there, may I not attain perfect enlightenment.''

The twelve theorems correspond, one by one, to structural features of this vow
and the Pure Land doctrine it generates.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{1.\ Universal access.}
Theorem~\ref{thm:UA}: the destination ($\mathcal{M}$) is realizable only if every region
of the configuration space has a path to it.
The vow: sentient beings \emph{of the ten directions}---all, without exception---shall
be born there.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{2.\ Indestructibility of the channel.}
Theorem~\ref{thm:interlock}: no consistent extension of the system can close a channel
that is already open.
The vow is unconditional once made: no subsequent force---karmic, cosmic, or
doctrinal---can revoke it.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{3.\ Persistence without sustained effort.}
Theorem~\ref{thm:persist}: a single moment of alignment at~$t_0$ suffices for permanent
residence on~$\mathcal{M}$.
The doctrine of non-retrogression (\emph{avaivartika}): once born in the Pure Land,
one does not fall back.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{4.\ Alignment, not force.}
Theorem~\ref{thm:scale}: capture is scale-invariant; direction, not magnitude, determines
access.
``Even ten recitations'' (\emph{naien shinen})---the vow specifies a threshold of
orientation, not of power.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{5.\ The global capture chain.}
Corollary~\ref{cor:GCC}: energy descent $\to$ phase-locking $\to$ $\mathcal{M}$, from
any initial state.
The threefold path: from suffering (cost accumulation), through trust (directional
alignment), to birth in the Pure Land (capture into~$\mathcal{M}$).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{6.\ Cost-independence.}
Corollary~\ref{cor:costindep}: prior cost $S[\psi_0]$ does not determine accessibility.
The vow does not discriminate by karmic debt.
Access depends on alignment, not on the weight of prior action.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{7.\ Infinite freedom within.}
Class~B motion along $\ker H$: zero cost, zero entropy, unconstrained, non-compactified.
The Pure Land as a realm of unobstructed activity (\emph{j\=\i yuz\=ai\/}):
infinite generative capacity without coherence loss.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{8.\ Exclusion is self-imposed.}
Lemma~\ref{lem:exclusion}: the sole obstruction is kernel-denial, which cannot persist on
any open set.
Shan-tao's commentary: it is the sentient being's own doubt, not any deficiency of the
vow, that obstructs birth.
The obstruction is geometric (misalignment), not external (selection).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{9.\ Unobstructed reach across all regions.}
Proposition~\ref{prop:merge}: kernel-connected non-collapsing completions are components
of a single~$\mathcal{M}$; the connecting Class~B motion is zero-cost, zero-entropy,
and meets no barrier.
The twelfth vow: ``If, when I attain buddhahood, my light should be limited, unable to
illuminate at the very least a hundred thousand nayutas of kotis of buddha-lands, may I
not attain perfect enlightenment.''
That is: light pervades the ten directions and all buddha-lands without obstruction
(\emph{k\=omy\=o hensh\=o jipp\=o sekai mushoges\=o}).
Class~B motion is the mathematical form of this unobstructed illumination.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{10.\ Smoothness of the destination.}
Proposition~\ref{prop:MB}: structural stability forces $\mathcal{M}$ to be a smooth
submanifold.
The Pure Land as a realm without roughness or obstruction---not by construction,
but by necessity.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{11.\ The spectral gap guarantees return.}
Proposition~\ref{prop:gap}: transverse perturbations are bounded away from the kernel;
departure from~$\mathcal{M}$ is restored.
Non-retrogression has a dynamical mechanism: not merely ``one does not fall back,'' but
``the system pulls one back.''

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{12.\ Uniqueness of the destination.}
Theorem~\ref{thm:unique}: there is exactly one non-collapsing completion.
There is one Pure Land, not many.
The vow does not say ``a land''; it says ``my land''---singular, unique, the only
non-collapsing option.

\bigskip
The identification is not a metaphor.
The vow and the twelve theorems share the same logical structure: a conditional whose
antecedent is the existence of the destination and whose consequent is the universality,
uniqueness, and indestructibility of access.
The mathematical version specifies what kind of access (kernel-aligned), what is invariant
(coupling strength), what is the sole obstruction (kernel-denial, which cannot persist),
and why the destination is unique (global non-isolation forces all critical structure
into~$\mathcal{M}$).

Whether this structural coincidence reflects identity or analogy is a question the
mathematics does not answer.
The mathematics says only: if the axiom holds, the twelve theorems follow.
